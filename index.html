About Fest

Wic-Can Fest offers a full schedule of workshops, lectures, demonstrations, concerts, and rituals for you to choose from, or you can spend your time getting reacquainted with old friends, making new ones, or enjoying solitude in the breathtaking natural surroundings.

We promote and encourage a Technology-Free environment to protect attendees' privacy and preserve the unique feel of our event, living as a Pagan village gathering.

Wic-Can Fest strives to provide an environment where one can become acquainted with the people of any given tradition, make face-to-face contact, and learn directly from those with the knowledge and wisdom to pass down their Tradition.

This is an opportunity to experience the feel and essence of the practice from various diverse paths, allowing both seekers and senior practitioners to make informed choices regarding their spiritual evolution and path. It is a way to learn and enrich their practice by making new connections and expanding their horizons.

Help us welcome our ancestors and honor the land spirits once again. Let us all gather together: Witches, Vikings, Shamans, Pirates, Fairies, Sprites, Pagans, and Heathens of all traditions. Come learn, celebrate, and revel in the magic!

BLESSED BE!

Festival Rules

The staff of Wic-Can Fest does not wish to curtail your enjoyment. Please remember that these rules have been put in place for your safety, the safety of the festival site, the safety of others, and for the general enjoyment of all. Those not respecting the festival's rules will be asked to leave!

General Rules:
  • Pets are not permitted on site. Registered service animals are excepted.
  • Conduct yourself with self-respect and respect for others.
  • All laws of Canada and the Province Of Ontario apply to those visiting the festival.
Site Rules:
  • Your vehicle must remain in the parking lot. There is no parking throughout the site. We request you keep your vehicle in the parking lot.
  • Do not dig or create any additional fire pits or collect ANY living vegetation for firewood.
  • All fires must be tended at all times and placed in above-ground fire vessels. Remember to put your fires out carefully. If you need to empty a fire bowl or other fire container, do so at a communal fire pit so the forest does not suffer after we leave!
  • Please only use the wood provided by the festival for cooking and camping purposes.
  • There shall be no washing of any kind, including hair, bodies, clothing, etc., in the lake.
  • Do not flush anything in the toilets except for human waste. Only pee is allowed in the urinals.
  • Anyone creating a hazard to the site or creating a situation that could have a potentially hazardous outcome will be asked to leave.
  • Smoking is strictly forbidden inside all buildings. Smoking can be conducted on your campsite or near the provided butt cans.
  • Each bed in the cabins is spoken for. Please do not put your things on another bed.
Attendance Rules:
  • All attendees are asked to sign up for at least one hour shift at Admin upon registration.
  • Please respect the rules and prerequisites (if any) of the diverse offerings, rituals, and special presentations onsite.
  • No sexually aggressive or inappropriate behavior will be tolerated.
  • You cannot wander through the site while skyclad.
  • Except at the discretion of the Festival, no electrically amplified instruments may be used.
  • Video cameras are not permitted and will be confiscated.
  • Do not photograph anyone without their prior consent. No ritual or group shots. As such, cameras and smart devices are not permitted at the main fire.
Attendees with Children Rules:
  • At least one parent or legal guardian MUST accompany any minor attending the festival.
  • Parents are responsible for their children at all times. Parents who do not, in the opinion of the festival Directors, exercise good parental care of their children will be asked to leave.
  • No child under age 12 may be left unattended near ANY body of water on the site.
  • If you are attending with children, you are expected to contribute to the kids' program center in any way possible.
  • Ensure your children know where you* are at all times since this is the first question they will be asked if they are found wandering unattended.
  • Ensure your child understands that our site has a zero-tolerance policy for bullying behavior and that respect for the elders, nature, each other, and all living things (including rocks) is essential.
Kitchen

The Main Feast is on Saturday night and, as always, is free of charge. At other times, you may purchase individual meals. Prices are outlined under Registration.


Security & Maintenance

If you notice any plumbing or site issues, please pop by the Admin and let us know. As we share the site with other groups, you must always wear your admission bracelet on your wrist during the festival. This will allow our staff members to keep non-festival goers from the fire pit, rituals, etc.

Frequently Asked Questions

Please reach us at board.wiccanfest@gmail.com if you cannot find an answer to your question.

Do I have to be Wiccan to attend Wic-Can Fest?

Of course not. Wic-Can Fest is open to everyone from all different communities worldwide and their friends.

Do I have to make a reservation to attend Wic-Can Fest?

No, you don’t have to make a reservation, but pre-registering is highly recommended. We have limited indoor cabin space and work on a first-come-first-served basis. Once the cabins are full, the remaining spaces left are for camping.

How early can I arrive on the first day?

You can not arrive prior to 3:00 pm. If you do, you will be responsible for paying the day fee as charged by the Outdoor Center (the owners of the site) in addition to the full cost of attending Wic-Can Fest.

Can I drink the water at the Festival?

Drinking water will be available in the kitchen. Feel free to fill up your drinking receptacles at the sink if no one is there. Please do not drink the water from the washroom faucets.

I plan to camp outdoors. Can I create a fire pit at my campsite if one doesn’t already exist?

No! It is absolutely forbidden to create any new fire pits. You must use the pre-existing ones or bring an above-ground metal basin, including its screen.

Is Wic-Can Fest child friendly?

Absolutely! Wic-Can Fest is a family-oriented festival. The festival site affords children a unique setting to explore their pagan heritage in a safe and constructive environment. Children under 12 MUST be accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or sitter. Children are not permitted to run wild on the site.

What is the weather like?

Weather varies from year to year. However, we typically experience daytime temperatures between 17-28 degrees Celsius, with overnight temperatures dropping to 10-19 degrees Celsius. We recommend checking the weather ahead of time and preparing for both rain and shine.

Are there shower facilities at Wic-Can Fest?

Full, hot showers and public bathrooms are available onsite, with outhouses in the camping areas.

Can I bring musical instruments to the festival?

Yes. Music is a big part of all of the pagan traditions. However, we ask that you do not bring any instruments that require electric amplification without asking us first.

Are pets allowed at Wic-Can Fest?

We are unable to accommodate pets at the site. Service animals are always welcome!

Can I bring a trailer?

Trailer spots are VERY limited. The site is not designed for trailer accommodations, so no water or hydro connections exist. If you want to bring a trailer, please email us at board.wiccanfest@gmail.com to ensure we have space for you! Depending on the size of the trailers, we can only accommodate 5-7 at a time.
